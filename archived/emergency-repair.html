Questions

Emergency repair FAQ

What are your emergency repair hours? expand_more

Text anytime, 24/7 — I'll see it. For in-person emergency repairs, I'm generally available from 8 AM to 10 PM, seven days a week, including holidays. If it's a true emergency outside those hours, text me anyway — I'll do my best to accommodate.

Is there an extra charge for nights or weekends? expand_more

No. Same prices, day or night, weekday or weekend. The only exception is if a part needs overnight or rush shipping — in that case, you can opt for expedited shipping at cost, but the repair labor stays exactly the same.

How fast can you fix my phone in an emergency? expand_more

Most screen replacements are done in under an hour. Battery swaps in 30–45 minutes. Water damage triage starts immediately. If I have the part in stock, many repairs are finished the same evening you text me.

What counts as an "emergency" repair? expand_more

Anything time-sensitive: a cracked screen before a trip, a dead phone with no backup way to communicate, water damage that needs immediate attention, a laptop that crashed before a deadline. If it feels urgent to you, it's urgent to me. No gatekeeping.

Do you do emergency repairs for tourists and visitors? expand_more

Absolutely. Sun Valley and Ketchum get a lot of visitors, and a broken phone on vacation is miserable. Just text me your situation and we'll figure out the fastest way to get your device repaired.

What if you don't have the part in stock? expand_more

I keep common parts (iPhone/Samsung screens, batteries, charging ports) in stock. For less common devices, I can usually get parts overnight or within 2 days. I'll be upfront about timing — no guessing games. If it's truly urgent, I can sometimes source parts from nearby suppliers the same day.
