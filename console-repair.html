Sound Familiar?

Common console problems I fix every week

gamepad

Joy-Con / Stick Drift

Your character moves on its own. Menus scroll randomly. Aiming is impossible. This is a hardware issue — recalibration won't fix it permanently.

desktop_access_disabled

No Display / HDMI Issues

Console powers on but TV says "No Signal." The HDMI port pins are bent, loose, or corroded. This is the #1 PS5 and Xbox repair I do.

thermostat

Overheating / Loud Fan

Console sounds like a jet engine or shuts off during gameplay. Dried thermal paste + dust buildup = CPU throttling. A deep clean fixes this.

disc_full

Won't Read Discs

Disc spins but game won't load, or the console won't accept the disc at all. Could be a dirty laser, bad roller, or failed disc drive motor.

battery_alert

Won't Charge / Dead Battery

Switch or Steam Deck won't turn on or dies in under an hour. Usually a worn battery or damaged USB-C port — both are replaceable.

broken_image

Cracked / Dead Screen

Switch or Steam Deck took a fall. Cracked glass, dead pixels, touch not responding. I replace the full display assembly for a factory-fresh result.

Console FAQ

Questions about game console repair

How much does Joy-Con drift repair cost? expand_more

Joy-Con drift repair is $29 per Joy-Con. I replace the entire analog stick module — not just a recalibration that'll drift again in a week. The new module is rated for millions of input cycles. Most drift repairs take under 45 minutes and come with a 40-day warranty. If both Joy-Cons drift, it's $29 each ($58 total).

Can you fix a PS5 that won't display on TV? expand_more

Almost always, yes. The PS5 HDMI port takes a beating — cables get yanked, kids trip over them, the connector loosens over time. HDMI port replacement is $59 and takes 1–2 hours. I desolder the damaged port and install a new one rated to HDMI 2.1 specs. I test with multiple cables and resolutions before returning it to you.

Do you repair all Switch models? expand_more

Yes — every model: Switch OLED (HEG-001), Switch V2 (HAC-001-01), original Switch (HAC-001), and Switch Lite (HDH-001). Common repairs include Joy-Con drift, screen replacement (LCD $69, OLED $99), charging port ($45), fan replacement ($39), game card slot, and rail connector issues.

Can you fix Xbox / PS5 controller drift? expand_more

Absolutely. Controller joystick replacement is $25–$35 depending on the controller (DualSense, DualShock 4, Xbox Wireless, Elite). I use Alps-equivalent modules rated for millions of cycles. The repair takes about 45 minutes. I'll test it in a game with precise aim requirements before giving it back.

Do you repair Steam Decks? expand_more

Yes! Valve designed the Steam Deck to be repairable and I take full advantage. Common repairs: joystick drift ($35), screen replacement ($79–$109 for LCD/OLED), battery swap ($59), SSD upgrade (from $49 + SSD cost), fan replacement ($39). The Steam Deck is honestly one of my favorite devices to work on — it's built for repair.

Is it worth repairing an older console like PS4 or Xbox One? expand_more

Usually yes. A PS4 thermal paste + deep clean ($39) can give it another 3–5 years — that's cheaper than one month of cloud gaming. HDMI port repair ($49) is a fraction of a used replacement. I'll give you an honest assessment — if the repair cost approaches what the console is worth, I'll tell you straight. No upselling, ever.
