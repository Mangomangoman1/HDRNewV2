Laptop FAQ

Questions about laptop repair

How much does a laptop screen replacement cost? expand_more

Laptop screen replacement starts at $99. MacBook Retina displays cost more than standard Windows laptop screens. I source the exact panel for your model — matte, glossy, touchscreen, or Retina — so it looks factory-fresh. I'll confirm the screen is the issue before starting.

Can you work on MacBooks with Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4)? expand_more

Yes. I work on all MacBook models including the latest Apple Silicon machines. Screen replacement, battery swap, keyboard repair, and SSD upgrades are all on the table. Apple Silicon Macs have soldered storage on newer models, but I can help with external solutions and data migration.

Will an SSD upgrade really make my old laptop faster? expand_more

Night and day difference. If your laptop still has a mechanical hard drive, swapping it for an SSD is the single best upgrade you can make. Boot times drop from 2–3 minutes to under 20 seconds. Apps open instantly. The whole machine feels new. Upgrade labor starts at $49, plus the cost of the SSD (typically $30–$80 for 500GB–1TB).

Can you recover files from a laptop that won't turn on? expand_more

Usually, yes. If the hard drive or SSD itself is still functional (which it often is even when the laptop is dead), I can pull the drive and recover your files. Data recovery starts at $79. I'll be upfront about chances before you commit.

How do I know if my laptop is worth repairing? expand_more

Good rule of thumb: if the repair costs less than 40–50% of a replacement, it's usually worth it. I'll always give you an honest assessment. Sometimes I tell people "this laptop has had a good run — put the repair money toward a new one." No hard sell, ever.

My kid spilled water on their Chromebook — can you fix it? expand_more

Bring it in ASAP. Turn it off, don't charge it, and get it to me as fast as possible. Liquid damage gets worse over time as corrosion sets in. I'll open it up, clean the board, and assess the damage. The sooner I see it, the better the odds. Same advice as phones — skip the rice.
