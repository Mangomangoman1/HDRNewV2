Packing Guide

How to pack your device for shipping

Proper packing = safe device. Here's the quick version.

smartphone

Phones & Tablets

  • check_circle Power off the device
  • check_circle Remove case and screen protector
  • check_circle Wrap in bubble wrap (2+ layers)
  • check_circle Use a small USPS Priority box
  • check_circle Fill gaps with packing paper
  • cancel Don't include chargers or accessories
laptop

Laptops

  • check_circle Power off and close the lid
  • check_circle Wrap in bubble wrap or foam
  • check_circle Use a box at least 2" larger on all sides
  • check_circle Pad all sides, especially corners
  • check_circle Include charger in a separate bag
  • cancel Don't ship in a laptop bag (not enough protection)
sports_esports

Game Consoles

  • check_circle Power off and unplug everything
  • check_circle Remove discs and cartridges
  • check_circle Wrap in bubble wrap
  • check_circle Original box is ideal if you have it
  • check_circle Joy-Cons: ship detached, wrap individually
  • cancel Don't include power cables unless asked
Questions

Mail-in FAQ

How does mail-in repair work? expand_more

Text or email us to describe your issue. We'll confirm the repair and give you a shipping address. Pack your device securely and ship it. We'll text you when it arrives, when the repair starts, and when we ship it back. Free return shipping included.

How long does the whole process take? expand_more

Most repairs are completed within 24 hours of receiving your device. With shipping each way, total turnaround is typically 3-5 business days depending on your location in Idaho. Boise/Nampa customers often get devices back in 2-3 days.

Is return shipping really free? expand_more

Yes. Return shipping via USPS Priority Mail is always free. You only pay for shipping your device to us. We can also provide a prepaid shipping label for an additional $8 if you'd prefer not to arrange your own shipping.

What if you can't fix my device? expand_more

Every repair starts with a thorough diagnosis. We'll explain what we found and give you a clear quote before any work begins. No surprise charges.

How should I pack my device? expand_more

Use a sturdy box with plenty of padding — bubble wrap, packing paper, or even crumpled newspaper works. Remove any cases or accessories. For phones, a small USPS Priority Mail box ($8-$9 at any post office) is perfect. Power off the device before shipping. See our full packing guide above.

Do you repair devices from outside Idaho? expand_more

Currently we focus on Idaho to keep turnaround times fast. If you're in a neighboring state (Montana, Wyoming, Utah, Oregon, Washington, Nevada), text us and we can probably work something out. Shipping is just a day or two longer.

Ready to get your device fixed?

Text us right now. Describe the problem. We'll have a quote and shipping instructions back to you within the hour.
