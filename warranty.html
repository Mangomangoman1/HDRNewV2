The Details

What's covered — and what's not

We keep it simple. No 15-page terms-and-conditions document. Here's the full picture.

check_circle

Covered

  • The specific repair we performed (screen, battery, port, etc.)
  • Replacement parts that fail or malfunction
  • Labor to re-do the repair
  • Same issue returning within 40 days
  • Manufacturing defects in replacement parts
  • Free return shipping for mail-in warranty claims
cancel

Not Covered

  • New damage: drops, spills, impacts, or cracks after repair
  • Software issues unrelated to the hardware repair
  • Modifications or repairs done by another shop afterward
  • Water or liquid damage that occurs after the repair
  • Normal wear and tear (batteries degrade over time)
  • Cosmetic issues not part of the original repair

The Honest Truth

Why I offer a warranty most shops don't

Most independent repair shops either don't offer a warranty or give you 30 days and hope you forget. I get it — warranty claims cost money. But here's the thing: if a repair fails within 40 days, that's on me. It means I either used a bad part or didn't do the job right.

A warranty isn't just about covering your risk — it's about me standing behind my work. If I'm not confident enough to guarantee a repair for 40 days, I shouldn't be doing the repair in the first place.

I source quality parts, I test every repair before handing it back, and I'd rather eat the cost of a redo than have someone walking around Hailey telling people I did a bad job. That's the reality of being a one-person shop in a small town — my reputation is everything.

— Samuel, Hailey Device Repair

Questions

Warranty FAQ

What exactly does the 40-day warranty cover? expand_more

It covers the specific repair performed — both parts and labor. If your new screen develops dead pixels, if your replacement battery starts draining abnormally, if your new charging port stops working — that's covered. Bring it back (or ship it) and we'll redo the repair at no charge.

What if I drop my phone and crack the new screen? expand_more

New physical damage isn't covered by the warranty — that's a new repair. But we'll still give you a fair price on fixing it again. And it won't affect the warranty on any other repairs we've done on that device.

Do I need to keep a receipt? expand_more

No. We keep records of every repair including the date, device, and what we fixed. Just text us and mention the repair — we'll look it up. Our text history with you is usually enough.

Can I get a warranty on a used part? expand_more

If we offer a used or refurbished part as a budget option (which we always disclose), the same 40-day warranty applies. We stand behind every part we install regardless of whether it's new, aftermarket, or refurbished.

What happens after 40 days? expand_more

After 40 days, the warranty expires — but you're still a customer. If something goes wrong, text us anyway. We'll be fair about pricing and prioritize getting you taken care of. Repeat customers are how a small business survives.
